Technology was not a choice; it was a natural path.
Since I was a child, my curiosity led me to explore the hidden codes of video games, to discover vulnerabilities in systems and to understand that, in this digital world, everything is malleable.

My first formal steps in computer science began with the intermediate level.
It was in that environment that I discovered cybersecurity and its infinite complexity.

What others saw as a set of rules, I understood as a constant challenge, a game of strategy where every decision counts. I am currently on my way to a degree in Networked Computer Systems Administration, with the aim of specialising in Cybersecurity. It's not just a degree, it's a commitment to a career that demands adaptability, creativity and, above all, a hacker mentality - in the purest sense of the term: question, understand and improve.

What caught me in this field was not the idea of protecting systems, but the fact that we are never really safe....

There is always a vulnerability, a backdoor, a human oversight that can be exploited. That makes this world so challenging and fascinating. I've learned that the best way to understand security is to think like an attacker. Every system, every network, every application has its Achilles heel.

I will always stick with this phrase... "every system is vulnerable until proven otherwise"
